TECH TITAN ELEVATE INKS STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP WITH MEDIA CONGLOMERATE GLOBAL-MEDIA

By Rajiv Mehta, Senior Tech Correspondent

AUSTIN, TX – In a move set to reshape the digital advertising landscape, Austin-based tech giant Elevate Inc. announced a landmark 10-year partnership with the London-based media powerhouse Global-Media Corp. on Tuesday.

The deal, valued at approximately $15 billion, will integrate Elevate's AI-driven data analytics with Global-Media's vast content network, which includes publications like The National Herald and streaming service Verve+.

The announcement was made jointly by Elevate CEO Elon Chase and Global-Media Chairwoman Dame Helena Wells from the stage of the Austin Convention Center.

"By combining our technological infrastructure with Dame Helena's incredible editorial assets, we are creating an ecosystem unlike anything seen before," said Chase, wearing his signature black leather jacket. "This will allow advertisers to reach audiences in Global-Media's properties with unprecedented precision, while protecting user privacy."

The partnership immediately raises questions for competitors like Menlo Park-based Meta and Beijing-based ByteDance. Analysts at Goldman Sachs released a note suggesting the deal "creates a formidable third pillar in the digital advertising duopoly."

Dame Helena Wells emphasized the cultural implications during the joint press conference. "We are not just feeding the machine; we are feeding the mind. Elevate gives us the tools to ensure that a reader in Mumbai sees the most relevant advertising alongside our journalism, which strengthens our business model and allows us to reinvest in newsrooms from New York to New Delhi."

However, the deal is expected to face intense scrutiny from regulators. European Union Competition Commissioner Margrethe Vestager released a brief statement indicating the merger would be "thoroughly reviewed for compliance with EU digital market standards."

The announcement caused Elevate's stock price to jump 8% in after-hours trading on the NASDAQ.
